Note:
  1. It is important that you do not use real credit card data during the performance of the tests, you can use credit card generators.
  2. Tests may be done only with credit cards, payment means such as cash or bank transfers cannot be tested by this method.
  3. Payment means in Peru cannot be tested, since these franchises handle 3D security.
 
 
 
EXAMPLES  
In the Authorization and Capture request you must:
  1. Ensure to send the prueba as true. Java: PayU.isTest = true; PHP: PayU::$isTest = true;
  2. Enter the APPROVED value in the name of the cardholder if you want the transaction to be approved or REJECTED if you want it to be rejected. Java: parameters.put(PayU.PARAMETERS.PAYER_NAME, "APPROVED"); PHP: PayUParameters::PAYER_NAME => "APPROVED"
  3. In the card number you must enter a valid card number, corresponding to the franchise sent in the request.

CHANGE TO PRODUCTION  
After you make all system testing and you verify that everything is working properly, you can make the change to the production server; where the actual transactions are processed.  
For this you should uncheck the transaction in test mode option which you will find in your administrative module.  

 
Additionally you must create payment forms with the following parameters relative to your account:  
merchantId: of your account
ApiKey: of your account
ApiLogin: of your account
accountId: of your account
test: false
 
Pointing to the production URLs:
Java 
PayU.paymentsUrl = "https://api.payulatam.com/payments-api/"; 
PayU.reportsUrl = "https://api.payulatam.com/reports-api/"; 
 
PHP 
Environment::setPaymentsCustomUrl("https://api.payulatam.com/payments-api/4.0/service.cgi"); 
Environment::setReportsCustomUrl("https://api.payulatam.com/reports-api/4.0/service.cgi"); 
Environment::setSubscriptionsCustomUrl("https://api.payulatam.com/payments-api/rest/v4.3/");
